# Disability 

Disabled persons shouldn't have to disclose to the public what their disability is and apologize for it like I am asked to do on a regular basis year over year in the tech industry. It's absolutely _disgusting_ frankly speaking and I have experienced it for _decades_. By my own hand, because for _decades_ I called myself Michael and for decades I was ashamed and terrified that I lived and survived the orphanages of Romania with a neurological motor disorder and _never_ and I mean **never** received _any early intervention services as a child of any kind_. Part of my name change included "Andrei rocks his head" and as Michael, I couldn't be seen or known that I rocked my head - out of risk and fear of literally _losing everything_ - being kicked out of the group - because I was damaged and defective - because of what was done to me - that I had literally _no control over_. 

As an adult, did I think I needed to get services on a regular basis so that I could have a modern paper trail documenting my orphanage experience, essentially mandating that its paperwork follow me for the rest of my life, or could I be a high functioning disabled person who coexisted with a neurological motor disorder in shame for decades and still chose to put his head down and build regardless? 

I never told Cisco that I rocked my head. I never told Oracle that I rocked my head. I never told WB Games that I rocked my head. I never told SurgePays that I rocked my head. Why should I? They never crossed the line to the point where they inflicted bodily injury onto me _because of it_. I told Beamable. **I had to.** Because getting woken up 10x a night caused my neck to rock 10x more than it normally would due to my disability and the excessive rocking coupled with high stress of unknown systems support in the middle of the night gave me multiple medical incidents that I requested reasonable accommodations for _multiple times_ throughout my employment at **all of these companies** that I worked with throughout my entire life. You see, Cisco rarely ever needed me in the middle of the night, but when they did, it definitely impacted my neurological motor disorder. I was ashamed that I had it and I was unwilling to get help for it. I can't do anything about it, other than make my life a little more comfortable than it has been in the past. I've lived with it for my entire life. 

My neck got seriously injured last year from my employment at Beamable. It's been 9 months now that I have been in recovery from the injury. The adult neck isn't designed to rock the way that I rocked in the orphanage. I tried to explain this to WB Games, but after my kids were born, I was essentially _thrown back into the orphanage_ since the pandemic - at least - in my environment. I had to listen to a young infant's cries for mama's milk and relive the experience that taught me after months of wondering whether she was every coming back for me, that crying did absolutely _nothing_. Instead of crying about it, I learned to rock my head instead. Instead of crying about what Beamable was doing to me, I was rocking my body to comfort myself. I could have cried and asked for mama's milk after the 2nd alarm caused minor irritation in my neck. I didn't. I am _so mad at myself for it_. I have lived with my rocking for 36 years until _Beamable_. 

I left Beamable prior to their acquisition by Skillz since they refused to take me off on-call support after I experienced a medical incident related to my neurological motor disorder that I developed in the orphanage in Romania. My requests for _reasonable accommodations_ were **flagrantly denied for 113 days** _until I quit_. Now that **Skillz** is running Beamable now, they now have accountability, just in a different way now. 

> _Professionally speaking, I wouldn't recommend other orphanage survivors work for **either** WB Games or Beamable in any capacity, based on **my experience**_.

I kept the receipts. _I wouldn't recommend working for them to my worst enemy._ Now, is the story and tech cool? Yes. But no disabled person should be subjected to 113 days of having reasonable accommodations rejected mocked and ignored. No disabled person should have their doctor's recommendations be flagrantly ignored dismissed and retaliated against over _months_. In good faith, I cannot and will not ever recommend **WB Games** or **Beamable** on these grounds. 

Not only is this conduct illegal for a good reason, but Beamable and WB Games operated above the law without accountability. The Sovereign in this case, the United States Government, is responsible for remediation and it has failed to act to protect its citizens. I observed **systemic failures** in the _Big Tech_ industry **at large** with regard to providing _reasonable accommodations_ for disabled persons _like myself_ given my complex neurological motor disorder. The common link between WB Games and Beamable is my direct manager Brandon. Therefore, holding Beamable accountable requires holding WB Games accountable, and for that matter, Oracle too. 

You see, Jackie Wegman ended my employment at Oracle because my _transition_ wasn't going according to _her expectations_. I told her that I wanted to identify as **Andrei** and not _Michael_ any longer, and **she assumed that I was trans**. I am an `is`/`be` - not trans. But to Jackie, that wasn't tolerable and she couldn't have it. So, I was laid off under the guise of the pandemic workforce reduction. I loved working at Oracle and with the Dyn team. I truly did. 

But when my kids were born and I was at WB Games, I was right back in the orphange again in my mind and I turned to vaping nicotine in order to cope with the stress of having to work on **p4gopher** while I was in the same building as children crying for mama's milk - in any capacity - all while my _direct manager_ was calling me a "dirty contractor". WB Games offered employment to me on the grounds that I cut my compensation by $30K per year from what _they were paying me as a contractor_ so I could be _considered full time_, but because I was asking for **reasonable accommodations** for my disability, they were **ignored and mocked during my one on ones with management**. Again, instead of crying for mama's milk, I rocked my head to comfort myself instead. My manager at WB Games caused a lot of head rocking in me, and when I moved teams, it didn't really lift - the behavior and conduct seemed to be **systemic**. So many people _simply_ have **zero fucks care** to give about persons with neurological motor disabilities that are high functioning. They see a high functioning adult and they judge them like they do not live with a neurological motor disorder from years of neglect and abandonment in an orphanage. 

The humility and the humanity in any and all of these people enmass that I had to work with since the pandemic, since having to increase my head rocking - incur injury in my lungs - and cope with the fact that I was reliving _hell on earth_ once again within my own physiology. Not only was my condition completely ignored, but while I was working at WB Games, I wasn't given sick days or vacation days and I was even expected to work while others took lavash vacation during the summer and fill in doing _their work_ because _I was the dirty contractor_. 

It made parting ways much easier for me, but it also made it much easier for me to say I will respond to a call if somebody needs help and wants to work with me, but I don't want to have to explain myself if I have a problem with my neurological motor disorder. That is what this is for. It explains it in a transparent manner that I can safely point to others and say "go read _this_ and you'll understand" - thus I can say it once, and not have to say it _over and over again_ later. 

The technology industry has **failed me** in more ways than I could ever possibly quantify. _That_ is that I am _ashamed_ of - not my motor disorder from the orphanage. The days of me blaming myself for the Romanian Government stealing me from a happy family so I could be internationally adopted are **over**.

I _very clearly_ was **not valued** either _as a person_ or _as a contributor_ while working with WB Games. In fact, Steven Flennory went out of his way to make me feel unwelcome.
