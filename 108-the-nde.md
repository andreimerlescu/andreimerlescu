# The NDE

WB ends their contracting arrangement after I said that I wasn't interested 
in continue working with Gary after the seminar that I was required to attend, 
where I explained to Gary my transition period during Oracle days, and ultimately
that I felt really uncomfortable during the seminar. It had nothing to do with 
anything technical, and it just really created extra emotional weight and stress 
onto me, that I already lived with challenges managing it that took me many years
of hard work to build, that it felt like Gary was willing to just throw away, making
me sit in on sessions like that, and how objecting to it was a form of bigotry.

I was so shaken by this. That's literally what happened. I was unboxing a bike 
for my kid's birthday and my hand slipped, the knife cut into my artery and my 
blood sprayed the bike's tires until I clamped the vein shut with my hand. It 
was all of sudden and instant. About 26 minutes later - almost to the minute, I
ended up arriving in Urgent Care and getting 2 stitches to close up the wound 
that stopped bleeding. Turns out, something happened during the car ride.

Well, I am holding my wrist, after its cut, and I'm fine for a bit, a little 
light headed, but fine, I didn't lose a lot of blood, and I was trying to get 
everybody in the car as fast as possible so I could be driven to the urgent care.
Well, we're in the car, and driving along the windy roads and I feel really 
light headed. I say out loud "I feel like I am going to pass out." And my ears
felt like they were vibrating, like they were physicall shaking. And well, I 
called out in that moment "Yeshua, please, help me!" and in that moment, I felt 
what can only be described, as strength in my hand where it had gone numb from 
prolongued holding, pressing into the wound and closing the bleeding. By the 
time that I embarked from the car to the urgent care waiting room, the bleeding
completely stopped. 

That evening, while stitched up, I was processing my life flashing before my
eyes. I was alone. I wanted to be huged by my mothers. Either one of them. But, 
circumstances in life, made it off the table and the cost of putting them on the
table, wasn't worth me accepting. That evening, I relived the good moments in my
childhood, mixed with some painful moments. I realized, during those moments, that 
I was pushed back. If my hand had slipped, I would have survived, yes, but the 
psychological impact would have been a much longer recovery than the initial 
inicident by itself. For whatever reason, God chose for me to experience that. 

The next day, I was a different man baking Challah bread. Something died in me 
that day. And I think it was the false identity of Michael, the final horcrux.
